Finding the perfect property or making a successful sale often depends on having the right real estate agent in your corner. Here's what to evaluate when making this critical decision.

## Experience Matters

Seasoned agents bring market understanding and established connections that newer agents simply haven't built yet. In commercial real estate — and especially in niche sectors like gas station brokerage — experience translates directly to better outcomes. Ask how many transactions they've closed in your specific asset class.

## Local Expertise

An agent with deep neighborhood knowledge provides insights on property values, traffic patterns, zoning regulations, and development trends that out-of-market brokers miss. For gas stations, local expertise means understanding municipal fuel storage regulations, environmental requirements, and competitive fuel pricing dynamics.

## Credentials and Licensing

Verify proper licensure through your state's licensing board. In commercial real estate, look for additional designations like CCIM (Certified Commercial Investment Member) or SIOR (Society of Industrial and Office Realtors) that demonstrate advanced training and commitment to the profession.

## Communication Skills

Effective agents maintain transparency, answer questions promptly, and keep you informed throughout the transaction. You should never have to wonder what's happening with your deal.

## Marketing Strategies

For sellers, understanding your agent's marketing approach is essential. Ask about their online presence, photography quality, buyer database, and how they plan to expose your property to the broadest qualified audience.

## Negotiation Skills

Both buyers and sellers benefit from agents skilled in securing favorable terms. Ask for specific examples of how they've navigated challenging negotiations in past transactions.

## References and Reviews

Past client feedback provides the most reliable performance insights. Ask for references from transactions similar to yours and check online reviews.

## Compatibility and Trust

Real estate transactions are stressful and time-intensive. You need an agent you trust, communicate well with, and feel comfortable relying on for major financial decisions.
