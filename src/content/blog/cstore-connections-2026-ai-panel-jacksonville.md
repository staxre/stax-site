*CStore Decisions* covered the second day of [CStore Connections](https://cstoredecisions.com/cstore-connections-ai-foodservice-and-more/) in Jacksonville, where STAX Real Estate Managing Partner Michael Salafia took the stage on the show's opening educational session — *AI at Work: Transforming the Future of the Convenience Store*. The panel was moderated by *CStore Decisions* Editor-in-Chief Erin Del Conte and brought together operators responsible for thousands of stores across the country.

## Operators at the Table

Michael joined Sorin Hilgen, Group Chief Digital Officer and U.S. CIO at EG America; Mike Wilson, Chief Operating Officer at Cubby's; and Scott Smith, Director of IT at Parker's Kitchen. The panel focused on how forward-thinking operators are using AI to improve foodservice execution, streamline labor management, and solve everyday operational challenges that have historically sat outside the reach of technology.

> "In the next 24 months, AI will completely reshape back office infrastructure for the convenience and gas industry." — Michael Salafia

## A Broker-Developer With Operator Credentials

What made the invitation notable is the seat Michael occupied on the panel. Most AI conversations at industry events feature either vendors selling a product or enterprise CIOs running large IT organizations. Michael spoke from a different vantage point — a broker and developer who also operates stores.

Through STAX, he advises and transacts on behalf of owner-operators acquiring, developing, and selling net-leased gas stations and convenience stores across Florida's top MSAs. Through sibling operating company Re-Up, he runs the same stores he helps other buyers acquire. That dual perspective — capital markets on one side, store-level P&L on the other — is a lens most industry discussions don't capture.

## How Re-Up Uses AI on the Ground

Re-Up specializes in the renovation and modernization of second-generation gas station c-stores. The playbook is straightforward: take a well-located but tired asset, upgrade the physical plant, install a modern operating system, and bring the store up to current-generation merchandising, foodservice, and labor standards.

AI sits inside that operating system. It helps Re-Up forecast demand, reduce shrink, optimize labor schedules against traffic patterns, and give store managers a clearer read on what's actually happening in their four walls. The panel conversation — centered on foodservice, labor, and daily problem-solving — maps directly onto the work Re-Up does every week at the store level.

## Free AI Tools for C-Store Operators

As part of the session, Re-Up highlighted its growing library of free AI tools for gas station and convenience store operators, now available at [reupfuel.com/ai](https://reupfuel.com/ai). The tools are designed for independent operators who want to start experimenting with AI without committing to an enterprise platform or a long implementation cycle. Contributing practical tools back to the operator community has been a core part of how Re-Up approaches the industry.

## What This Means for STAX Buyers

For buyers working with STAX, the operator perspective matters. Underwriting an owner-user gas station in a top Florida MSA is not just a cap rate exercise — it's a judgment about whether the site can be operated profitably through a cycle that now includes EV adoption, foodservice expansion, labor pressure, and AI-enabled efficiency gains. STAX continues to deliver stabilized owner-user gas station opportunities across Florida's top MSAs, and increasingly those deals are underwritten by the same operator intuition Michael brought to the panel in Jacksonville.

## Continued Industry Recognition

Being asked to speak alongside CIOs and COOs from some of the country's largest c-store platforms reflects how the industry is thinking about AI — not as a future question but as a present-tense operational tool. STAX and Re-Up will continue to contribute to that conversation through speaking engagements, tool releases, and the deals and renovations that turn the conversation into real store-level performance.
